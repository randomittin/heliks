why heliks?

why do you care?

But to reach that level, there are certain number of exams that we doctors have to pass, may it be USMLE for US seats for PG or PLAB for the UK students.
But how?

Eventually we lose motivation, and we seek some sort of “appraisal” from the ones who really understand how hard these exams could be on us.

The Constitution recognizes marriage as a liberty right protected by the right to privacy. This right was extended first for interracial marriages i (1967) and then to same-sex marriages (2015).

The constitutional right to marry was established during Loving v. Virginia, yet it wasn't until Roe v. Wade’s that marriage became protected by the right to privacy as well. In addition, it wasn’t until Obergefell v. Hodges (a case that heavily cited Roe) that this right extended to same-sex marriage and considered marriage not only “an orderly pursuit of happiness” but also; “like choices concerning contraception, family relationships, procreation, and childrearing, all of which are protected by the Constitution, decisions concerning marriage are among the most intimate that an individual can make.”
Landmark Cases:
Loving v. virginia (1967)
zablocki v. redhail (1978)
obergefell v. hodges (2015)
“America has taken one more step toward the promise of equality enshrined in our Constitution, and I'm humbled to be part of that.”
jim obergefell

It is the right of family members to maintain relationships with other family members and decide whether or not to bear children without government interference. Despite the legalization of same-sex marriage, same-sex couples are still treated differently under law when it comes to the application of the presumption of parentage i.

The central holdings in Roe v. Wade assisted in strengthening and explaining the pre-existing constitutionality of family decision making and relationships by connecting it to other rights already acknowledged by the Supreme Court, such as contraception, sexual rights and marriage. This allowed cases after Roe to assert that liberty does encompass decisions in family life i. Cases like Pavan v. Smith have expanded the presumption of parentage to all spouses of gestational parents, making it accessible for many more members of the LGBTQ community and giving greater recognition to parenthood based on intent rather than biology.
Landmark Cases:
meyer v. nebraska (1923)
Pierce v. the society of sisters (1925)
Moore v. city of east cleveland (1977)
pavan v. smith (2017)

It is a person’s right to be secure in their “persons, houses, papers and affects” against unreasonable violations and searches. Since Casey v. Planned Parenthood i, the Supreme Court has not decided cases that build upon the right to bodily integrity and it is still unclear how this right may restrict government action in the forms of physical searches and punishment.
‍
‍Bodily integrity was considered a liberty before Roe v. Wade. Rochin v. California addressed the fact that while the due process clause did not spell out specifics, it protects “personal immunities” that “are so rooted in the traditions and conscience of our people to be ranked as fundamental.” Casey vs. Planned Parenthood (which relied heavily on advancements made by Roe and cited Rochin) is considered a foundational case for developing this liberty. Following the advancements made to the privacy right by Roe, the Supreme Court extended “privacy” to include the right to appoint a healthcare proxy i or refuse medical treatment. Roe (and Casey) developed the framework to assist courts in assessing when the government’s actions impede on this personal autonomy.
Landmark Cases:
rochin v. california (1952)
washington v. harper (1990)
casey v. planned parenthood (1992)
“bodily autonomy is the foundation upon which all other human rights are built.”
dr. natalia kanem
